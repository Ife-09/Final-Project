Food

Here are some tasty traditional dishes from our top three countries!

Thailand

Rice is the anchor of almost every Thai meal. Everything from stir-fried vegetables to grilled pork, curries, and soups is meant to be eaten with either steamed rice or sticky rice.

Pad Thai

Ad Thai, phat Thai, or phad Thai, is a stir-fried rice noodle dish commonly served as a street food in Thailand as part of the country's cuisine

Tom Yum Soup

Tom yum or tom yam is a family of hot and sour Thai soups. The strong, hot, and sour flavors make it very popular in Thai cuisine.

Morocco

Moroccan cuisine is usually a mix of Arab, Berber, Andalusi, and Mediterranean cuisines, with minimal European (French and Spanish) and sub-Saharan influences

Chicken Pastilla

A savory chicken pie made with crispy, flaky phyllo dough and a tender shredded chicken filling, seasoned with warm North African flavors including Ras El Hanout, ginger, and cinnamon

CousCous

Authentic Moroccan couscous is a colorful dish made with perfectly cooked couscous topped with lamb, a variety of stewed veggies and a flavorful broth

Dubai

Meat, fish, and rice are the staple foods of Emirati cuisine. Lamb and mutton are the more favored meats rather than goat, beef, and camel meat. Dates are usually consumed with meals. Popular beverages are coffee and tea, which can be supplemented with cardamom, saffron, or mint to give it a distinct flavor.

Luqaimat

Luqaimat (or Awamat) are middle eastern fried dough fritters dunked in simple syrup. They are crunchy on the outside and soft and airy on the inside; so addictive and so easy to make! Follow this step by step recipe for the perfect dough and frying method.

Knafeh

Knafeh is a Middle Eastern dessert with a shredded filo dough crust, a sweet cheese filling, topped with a rose water flavored simple syrup.
